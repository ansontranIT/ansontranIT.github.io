Ideal Job

Job Title CISO

Description

This position is part of the C-suite level of executives that are responsible for the defence and safety of the company’s information and technologies, and consumer and client details. They are required to respond to incidents, establish appropriate standards and controls, manage security technologies, and direct the establishment and implementation of policies and procedures. As such the CISO is also responsible for information-related compliance. Being part of the C-suite level, the CISO works with other executives to make sure the company is growing and providing profit to the shareholders.

Why does this appeal to you?

The reason why this appeals to me is that it is a very challenging role that not only requires technical ability but also business acumen and the ability to manage people. The responsibility to steer the direction of security in an organisation is amazing as you are making something worthwhile and impactful to people. Something to be proud of, especially when going from zero to one hundred. Just seeing the impact of your changes draws me into the role and I hope that one day I will be able to impact the lives of people in a meaningful way through security.

What skills are required for this position?

From what I’ve researched and from the job listing to be a CISO you need to have at the very least 7- 12 years of experience within the field of cyber-security, 5 of those years holding positions of management such as IT manager/Security Manager and also holding certifications such as CISSP and CISM. This is a senior position of cybersecurity that requires both business acumen and technical skills.

For education people in this role are expected to have a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science or a related field. There is also an increasing trend of having a Masters Degree with a security focus, such as our RMIT’s Master of Cyber Security. Some sources also state that having a business degree, such as an MBA, would be extremely useful for the position. Professional Certifications include the aforementioned CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional) and the CISM (Certified Information Security Manager).

The typical skills that are required for the business side of a CISO include project management, financial management, and people management. CISO is expected to help with regulatory compliance so knowing them is needed. An example would be knowing the ASIC (Australian Securities & Investments Commission) cybersecurity guidelines.

Whilst on the technical aspect, there is a huge list of expected skills. Similar to other high-level tech executives you would need advanced levels of programming and knowledge of system administration. You should also have knowledge of security-focused technologies such as DNS, routing, authentication, VPNs, DOS and DDOS. You would also need to know coding practices, ethical hacking and threat modelling, and firewall and intrusion detection/prevention protocols.

However, this balance is very subjective and generally speaking the skills of the CISO is determined by what the company needs. Smaller companies may value more technical CISOs while larger international bodies may want someone with a holistic skillset balancing both business and technical skill in security.
