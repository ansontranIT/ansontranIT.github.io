Ideal Job

Job Title CISO

Description

This position is part of the C-suite level of executives that are responsible for the defence and safety of the company’s information and technologies, and consumer and client details. They are required to respond to incidents, establish appropriate standards and controls, manage security technologies, and direct the establishment and implementation of policies and procedures. As such the CISO is also responsible for information-related compliance. Being part of the C-suite level, the CISO works with other executives to make sure the company is growing and providing profit to the shareholders.

Why does this appeal to you?

The reason why this appeals to me is that it is a very challenging role that not only requires technical ability but also business acumen and the ability to manage people. The responsibility to steer the direction of security in an organisation is amazing as you are making something worthwhile and impactful to people. Something to be proud of, especially when going from zero to one hundred. Just seeing the impact of your changes draws me into the role and I hope that one day I will be able to impact the lives of people in a meaningful way through security.

What skills are required for this position?

From what I’ve researched and from the job listing to be a CISO you need to have at the very least 7- 12 years of experience within the field of cyber-security, 5 of those years holding positions of management such as IT manager/Security Manager and also holding certifications such as CISSP and CISM. This is a senior position of cybersecurity that requires both business acumen and technical skills.

For education people in this role are expected to have a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science or a related field. There is also an increasing trend of having a Masters Degree with a security focus, such as our RMIT’s Master of Cyber Security. Some sources also state that having a business degree, such as an MBA, would be extremely useful for the position. Professional Certifications include the aforementioned CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional) and the CISM (Certified Information Security Manager).

The typical skills that are required for the business side of a CISO include project management, financial management, and people management. CISO is expected to help with regulatory compliance so knowing them is needed. An example would be knowing the ASIC (Australian Securities & Investments Commission) cybersecurity guidelines.

Whilst on the technical aspect, there is a huge list of expected skills. Similar to other high-level tech executives you would need advanced levels of programming and knowledge of system administration. You should also have knowledge of security-focused technologies such as DNS, routing, authentication, VPNs, DOS and DDOS. You would also need to know coding practices, ethical hacking and threat modelling, and firewall and intrusion detection/prevention protocols.

However, this balance is very subjective and generally speaking the skills of the CISO is determined by what the company needs. Smaller companies may value more technical CISOs while larger international bodies may want someone with a holistic skillset balancing both business and technical skill in security.

Project Idea

Network Security RMIT Case Study

Overview

This project is a Cyber Security project aiming for students to understand the impact of cyber attacks on a network. Through researching security policies and actions taken during the aftermath of the attack to prevent future incursion, and then compiling all their research into creating a website to educate others.

Motivation

Cybercrimes are highly dangerous and as time goes past cyber security is going to be more and more important. We are very well connected in this day and age. With the help of technology doing things has never been easier. However, this has provided a new venue for people to exploit. Cybersecurity is an arms race. The more better developed defences are the more better developed attacks are. People need to be educated on why and how attacks occur and how they can contribute to the safety of their cyberspace. The first step would be to educate people on cyberattacks and how they could mitigate the risks of it happening.

Description

Students will first be researching RMIT security standards, security systems, and authentication protocols with permission and supervision from an appropriate staff member. They will interview staff members and department heads to ascertain specific security concerns and issues and provide a post-security analysis on the recent RMIT cyber attack.

Students need to create reports detailing all the ins and outs of the procedures, standards, endpoints etc. They will develop a risk assessment report for the case study. For each threat, the report should describe the risk, vulnerabilities and value. Along with the impact and likelihood of occurrence and control recommendations.

Key questions we need to ask are:

  • What are RMIT's most important information technology assets?
  • What data breach would have a major impact on RMIT?
  • What are the relevant threats and the threat sources?
  • What are the internal and external vulnerabilities?
  • What is the impact if those vulnerabilities are exploited?
  • What is the likelihood of exploitation?
  • What cyber attacks, cyber threats, or security incidents could impact the ability of the university to function?
  • What is the level of risk that RMIT can take?

After research students would begin creating a website with the purpose of educating students on digital safety through using the recent RMIT cyber attack as a case study. Providing risk analyses and security assessments based on what they find. Showcasing the impact of the attack and recommending actions that students could do in order to prevent any future cases happening and/or mitigating the risks of them compromising their data. End deliverable would be a functioning website that educates people on the effects of cyber attacks and how to potentially mitigate them.

For the website creation. Firstly, the goal of the website is to be able to educate people on the impact of a cyber attack and ways that could be done to mitigate the effect. From that we would need to define a scope of what the website functions can be. I’m thinking around 3-6 pages depending on what information the team deems relevant for the website.

Next we would design a sitemap detailing the information each page would contain. At this stage we would discuss what sort of features would be implemented and design the look of the website on paper using wireframes. We would also begin to create the mood board to define the design direction of the website.

Afterwards is the meat of the process. This stage we would begin to use the research data we’ve accumulated and begin to create the content of the site. We would write the content in a concise way so non-technical people may understand it. The goal of the website is to educate all people. Royalty free imagery or self-made imagery will be considered and implemented with purpose to enhance the content.

Lastly we would need to test the website to make sure that everything is working and that there are no bugs to squash. Afterwards we would either launch the website through the use of github.io or using a purchased domain name and web provider.

This would contribute to introducing students into cybersecurity through a real world case study, and educate them on the effects and how to mitigate them. As well as furthering their web design and programming skills with the main deliverable.
